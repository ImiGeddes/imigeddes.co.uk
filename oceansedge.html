OCEANS EDGE

Creating a website that inspires people to explore the beautiful wilderness in Newfoundland

UX & UI Designer

THE TASK

Ocean’s Edge approached me to create a website for a new luxury retreat they were opening in Newfoundland. The website was principally to be used as a marketing tool to attract potential customers, and to encourage users to contact the client to get a quote. They had a logo already, however the rest of the design decisions were up to me.

RESEARCH

I kicked off the project by doing a lot of domain research to be inspired by other websites for luxurious accomodation. I compiled a selection of websites and conducted a gut test with my client, which gave me an understanding what they wanted from the project. I find gut tests to be really useful, as clients are often better at expressing their opinions when they can see something visual.

I then sent out an online survey. The purpose of this was to understand what was important to users when deciding to book a holiday. I found that the 3 most important features users wanted to see when looking at accomodation websites were:

1. List of ammenities

2. Pictures of the facilities

3. Customer reviews

THE WHY

From my user research and looking into Newfoundland and the tourism industry, as well as speaking to the client I devised a ‘why’ statement that encompassed Ocean’s Edge:

To allow travellers to explore the wilderness while living in luxury

DESIGN DIRECTIONS

I used my ‘why’ statement to act as a base for two different design directions. For each, I started by creating a mood for each direction with different mood words, and then different design language. I used this to make key design decisions on colours, fonts and iconography. Below you can see both directions shown by the mood boards and style tiles.

I presented both directions to the client, and they chose the ‘outdoor elegance’ direction, however they wanted to use some of the lighter beige colours from the rustic charm colour palette.

PLANNING

With the design direction finalised, I went on to planning the structure of the site. I ensured I kept the key users in mind throughout the process; they are affluent, older middle-aged, adventurous nature-lovers. The user is not a technology native, or someone that spends a lot of time online. The site must therefore be simple and straight-forward. However, as Ocean’s Edge is a luxury retreat, the website must provide enough information to show users that a trip there will be worth the money.

I kept the site map very simple, with a home page, three pages to show off the key things that Ocean’s Edge has to offer and the contact page.

DESIGN

With the site map planned out I created wireframes for the site and drafted the copy. I did some user testing with the wireframes to ensure the site gave enough information to entice users in, without being overwhelmed. From testing, I realised that I needed to make the contact page more obvious and easy to access, as this is the end-goal that we wanted all users to get to.

I sent the wireframes to the client so they could read over the copy and suggest changes, without being distracted by the visual elements. I made iterations to the layout and the copy from feedback I received, then added the UI elements and photos. I was lucky that Ocean’s Edge had a whole library of great pictures to choose from, which I ensured were the focal point of the website, as they showed off how beautiful Ocean’s Edge is and allows the user to imagine themselves there.

REFLECTIONS

This was my first solo client project, where I was responsible for the full UX and UI. I found this to be a daunting challenge at first - I am normally a very collaborative designer, and I love brainstorming in groups to find creative solutions. Without other designers to bounce ideas off of, I found that user testing was even more important as I was able to get lots of ideas while talking to users.

The second challenge was that the key users for Ocean’s Edge are not generally technically savvy. I had to really empathise with the user and ensure that what I was designing met their needs.

This project taught me to be confident with my designs - when working alone you need to back yourself and your design decisions. I found that constantly reminding myself of the end user and the ‘why’ statement when making decisions allowed me to be confident in what I was doing.
